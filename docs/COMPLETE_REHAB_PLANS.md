## ‚úÖ **Fully Implemented Rehab Plans** (6 Total)

### **KNEE INJURIES**

#### 1. **ACL Tear (Anterior Cruciate Ligament)**
- **Duration:** 24 weeks (6 months)
- **Phases:** 4
- **Total Exercises:** 15+
- **Severity:** Severe
- **Recovery Time:** 6-12 months

**Phase Structure:**
1. **Immediate Post-Op / Protection Phase** (Weeks 1-2)
   - Ankle pumps, quad sets, heel slides
   - Focus: Reduce swelling, protect graft
   
2. **Motion & Strength Phase** (Weeks 3-6)
   - Straight leg raises, mini squats, hamstring curls, stationary bike
   - Focus: Achieve full ROM, improve quad strength
   
3. **Progressive Strengthening Phase** (Weeks 7-12)
   - Step-ups, single leg balance, wall squats, calf raises
   - Focus: Build strength, improve proprioception
   
4. **Return to Sport Phase** (Weeks 13-24)
   - Box jumps, lateral bounds, agility drills, single leg squats
   - Focus: Regain power, pass functional tests

---

#### 2. **MCL Tear (Medial Collateral Ligament)**
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Phases:** 3
- **Total Exercises:** 8
- **Severity:** Moderate
- **Recovery Time:** 4-8 weeks

**Phase Structure:**
1. **Pain and Swelling Control** (Week 1-2)
   - Heel slide, wall heel slide
   - Focus: Reduce pain, maintain gentle mobility
   
2. **Quadriceps Activation and Strength Restoration** (Week 2-4)
   - Quad set, short arc quad activation
   - Focus: Regain quad control, improve stability
   
3. **Hip Strength and Functional Stability** (Week 5-8)
   - Banded hip abduction, hip flexion with SLR, hip adduction, lateral step-up
   - Focus: Improve hip stability, functional recovery

**Key Features:**
- Detailed instructions with foam roller modifications
- Hold times specified (6 seconds)
- Progressive difficulty with equipment options

---

#### 3. **Meniscus Tear**
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Phases:** 3
- **Total Exercises:** 10
- **Severity:** Moderate
- **Recovery Time:** 6-12 weeks

**Phase Structure:**
1. **Acute Phase** (Weeks 0-2)
   - Heel slide, quadriceps set, ankle pumps
   - Focus: Reduce pain, begin motion, activate quads
   
2. **Early Strengthening Phase** (Weeks 2-4)
   - Straight leg raise, hip abduction, hip adduction, isometric hamstring curl
   - Focus: Develop quad/hamstring/hip strength
   
3. **Functional Strengthening & Control** (Weeks 4-6+)
   - Mini squats (~60¬∞), lateral step-up
   - Focus: Restore full strength, balance, movement control

**Key Features:**
- Specific tempo instructions (2 sec up, 2 sec down)
- Common mistakes highlighted
- Tips for proper form (e.g., "keep hip pushed forward")

### **ANKLE INJURIES**

#### 4. **Lateral Ankle Sprain (Inversion Sprain)**
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Phases:** 4
- **Total Exercises:** 7
- **Severity:** Mild to Moderate
- **Recovery Time:** 2-8 weeks

**Phase Structure:**
1. **Acute / Mobility Phase** (Week 1-2)
   - Ankle dorsiflexion mobility, ankle strengthening (eversion band)
   - Focus: Reduce swelling, restore mobility
   
2. **Strengthening & Balance Phase** (Week 3-4)
   - Calf raise exercise, proprioceptive control (clock reaches)
   - Focus: Improve calf/ankle strength, reintroduce balance
   
3. **Functional Strength Phase** (Week 5-6)
   - Single-leg squat, forward lunge
   - Focus: Strengthen functional patterns, single-leg control
   
4. **Advanced / Return-to-Activity Phase** (Week 7-8)
   - Hop to landing
   - Focus: Restore dynamic stability, prevent re-injury

**Key Features:**
- Wall-based dorsiflexion assessment
- Clock reaches for proprioception (12, 9, 6 o'clock)
- Progressive jump height protocols

---

#### 5. **Medial Ankle Sprain (Eversion Sprain / Deltoid Ligament)**
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Phases:** 3
- **Total Exercises:** 10
- **Severity:** Moderate
- **Recovery Time:** 3-8 weeks (longer than lateral)

**Phase Structure:**
1. **Acute / Mobility Phase** (Week 1-2)
   - Ankle pumps, ankle circles
   - Focus: Reduce pain, maintain mobility, prevent stiffness
   
2. **Strengthening & Balance Phase** (Week 3-5)
   - Single-leg balance, ankle inversion band, ankle eversion band, heel raise off step, ankle dorsiflexion wall support
   - Focus: Restore inner ankle strength, improve proprioception
   
3. **Functional / Return-to-Sport Phase** (Week 6-8)
   - Double-leg jump, lateral bound, single-leg hops
   - Focus: Build power, landing control, confidence

**Key Features:**
- Tripod foot concept for balance
- Both inversion and eversion band work
- Mirror checks for symmetry
- Progressive plyometric protocol

---

#### 6. **High Ankle Sprain (Syndesmotic Sprain)**
- **Duration:** 8 weeks (often longer than standard sprains)
- **Phases:** 3
- **Total Exercises:** 6
- **Severity:** Severe
- **Recovery Time:** 6-12 weeks

**Phase Structure:**
1. **Acute Phase** (Week 1-2)
   - Elevated ankle pumps, ankle circles
   - Focus: Reduce swelling, maintain circulation, protect syndesmosis
   
2. **Subacute / Strengthening Phase** (Week 2-4)
   - Progressive weight bearing, double-leg calf raises
   - Focus: Gradually restore weight-bearing, strengthen calf
   
3. **Functional / Return to Activity Phase** (Week 4-6)
   - Proprioceptive control (clock reaches), glute bridge
   - Focus: Build stability, coordination, supporting strength

**Key Features:**
- Emphasis on elevated exercises initially
- Very gradual weight-bearing progression (5-10% ‚Üí 100%)
- 50-50 weight distribution focus
- Longer recovery acknowledgment
